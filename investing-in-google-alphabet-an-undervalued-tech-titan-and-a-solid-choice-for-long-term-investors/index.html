Investing in Google (Alphabet): An Undervalued Tech Titan and a Solid Choice for Long-Term Investors

Andrew Zhu February 19, 2025

1. Introduction

In the global tech stock market, Google (Alphabet) remains a central focus for investors. This technology giant, with its leading positions in search engines, artificial intelligence, cloud computing, and digital advertising, has consistently demonstrated robust profitability. However, market fluctuations in its valuation often lead to investor uncertainty. This article will delve into the investment value of Google through various dimensions, including financial data, market competitiveness, valuation levels, and investment strategies, while also exploring investment opportunities within the current market environment.

2. Google (Alphabet) Business Empire Overview: Diversified Businesses Building Growth Engines

When we talk about Google, we’re not just referring to the search engine we use daily. Google is, in fact, part of Alphabet, a sprawling and diversified technology conglomerate. Let’s uncover the layers of Alphabet’s business, much like charting a treasure map, to reveal that it’s far more than a search engine company – it’s a tech juggernaut propelled by multiple growth engines:

2.1. Core Engine: Digital Advertising – The Source of Cash Flow

Undeniably, digital advertising remains Alphabet’s most crucial revenue pillar and its continuous source of cash flow. Consider this: whether you are searching for information on Google, watching videos on YouTube, or using services like Gmail and Google Maps, ubiquitous advertisements are silently contributing to revenue. Google Search and YouTube, these twin “traffic harvesting machines,” firmly dominate the global digital advertising market with their massive user base and precise ad targeting technology. This segment functions like a perpetual money-printing machine, providing the Alphabet group with steady cash flow to support innovation and expansion in cutting-edge fields.

2.2. Rising Star: Cloud Computing (Google Cloud) – The Strong Support of the Second Growth Curve

In recent years, the cloud computing business, Google Cloud, has increasingly become a robust support for Alphabet’s second growth curve. The cloud computing market is a commanding height in future technology competition and a cornerstone of enterprise digital transformation. While Google Cloud started somewhat later, it is rapidly catching up to industry leaders, leveraging Google’s strong technological foundation and continuous investment in global infrastructure. From Infrastructure-as-a-Service (IaaS) to Platform-as-a-Service (PaaS) and Software-as-a-Service (SaaS), Google Cloud has built a comprehensive cloud service ecosystem covering computing, storage, databases, artificial intelligence, machine learning, and more. It’s capable of offering comprehensive cloud solutions for enterprise clients. With the accelerating pace of global enterprise digital transformation, the cloud computing market holds immense potential, and Google Cloud is poised to become a significant driver of Alphabet’s future revenue growth.

2.3. Pioneers in Cutting-Edge Technologies: AI (DeepMind), Autonomous Driving (Waymo), etc. – Future-Oriented Strategic Layout

Alphabet’s ambitions extend far beyond search engines and cloud computing. In frontier technology domains like artificial intelligence (DeepMind), autonomous driving (Waymo), and life sciences (Verily), Alphabet has also committed substantial resources for long-term strategic deployments. While these forward-looking businesses may not contribute significant revenue in the short term, they represent Alphabet’s future-oriented strategic investments and are crucial for building its future technology ecosystem and maintaining long-term competitiveness.

  • DeepMind: As a leader in artificial intelligence, DeepMind possesses deep technical reserves in algorithms, models, and computing power. Projects like AlphaGo and AlphaFold, developed by DeepMind, have astounded the world, demonstrating the vast potential of AI technology. Although DeepMind is currently focused on technological R&D, with commercialization paths still under exploration, its AI technology reserves will provide a powerful technological engine for Alphabet’s future business development.

  • Waymo: Autonomous driving technology is a revolutionary force for the future of transportation. Waymo, as a pioneer in autonomous driving, holds a leading position in technological research and development and real-world road testing. While the commercial rollout of autonomous driving technology still requires time, once the technology matures, Waymo is expected to usher in explosive growth in areas such as Robotaxis and logistics transportation, opening up new growth spaces for Alphabet.

Summary: Alphabet is not a company with a singular business; it is a diversified and well-balanced technology conglomerate. Digital advertising provides steady cash flow, cloud computing contributes rapidly growing revenue, and cutting-edge technology businesses represent future development potential. This diversified business structure not only reduces single-business risks but also provides multiple growth drivers for the company. This is precisely what ensures Alphabet, as a tech giant, can maintain continuous innovation and market competitiveness. For investors, a diversified business layout implies lower investment risk and broader growth prospects.

3. In-depth Analysis of Market Competitiveness: The Iron Throne of Search Engines and Re-Consolidation Amidst the AI Wave

Next, we focus on Alphabet’s market competitiveness, particularly its core business – search engines – and the competitive landscape amidst the AI wave:

3.1. Search Engine Dominance: An Unshakeable Moat

“Just Google it” has become a global catchphrase for internet users, itself an endorsement of Google’s search engine dominance in the global market. Data shows that Google’s search engine commands a staggering nearly 90% share of the global market, effectively monopolizing the internet traffic gateway. YouTube, as the world’s second-largest search engine, also boasts a vast user base and immense commercial value. These two platforms constitute Alphabet’s most valuable assets, with advertising revenue forming Alphabet’s primary source of cash flow.

Google’s search engine dominance is not a matter of chance; it is the result of long-term technological accumulation, user habit formation, and the compounding effects of network effects. It boasts:

  • Leading Search Technology: Google has long maintained a leading edge in search engine algorithms, indexing technologies, and natural language processing, enabling it to provide users with more accurate, faster, and more comprehensive search results.
  • Massive Data Resources: Google possesses the world’s largest internet data index library, capable of covering virtually all internet information, a feat unmatched by competitors.
  • Strong User Stickiness: Over time, users have developed a habit of using the Google search engine. Once this habit is formed, it is very difficult to change easily.
  • Powerful Brand Effect: The brand “Google” itself represents authority, professionalism, and reliability, enjoying high brand recognition and trust among users.

These factors collectively form Google’s powerful search engine moat, making its dominant position difficult to shake.

3.2. AI Era Challenges: Strategic Responses in a New Competitive Landscape

Undeniably, the rise of AI search tools, represented by ChatGPT in recent years, has presented certain challenges to traditional search engine businesses. Users are beginning to experiment with AI tools for information retrieval, and market discussions about whether “AI search will subvert traditional search” are emerging. The advent of AI search indeed, to some extent, changes the competitive landscape of the search market, but to prematurely conclude that Google’s search engine dominance is in imminent danger is somewhat exaggerated.

The author believes that the rise of AI search tools presents both challenges and opportunities for Google.

  • Challenges: The emergence of AI search tools may divert some of Google’s search traffic, especially in certain scenarios where AI tools can provide more convenient and personalized ways to retrieve information.
  • Opportunities: Google itself is also vigorously developing AI technology and applying it to search engines, cloud computing, advertising, and various other business areas. Google possesses a top-tier AI research institution like DeepMind, with a clear first-mover advantage in AI technology accumulation and talent reserves. Google is fully capable of leveraging AI technology to further enhance the user experience of its search engine, solidify its market leadership, and even open up new growth spaces.

Faced with the challenges of the AI era, Google is not sitting idly by but is actively embracing change and taking the initiative. For example, Google is deeply integrating AI technology into its search engine, launching new features such as “Search Generative Experience (SGE),” attempting to combine traditional search with AI technology to create a smarter, more efficient next-generation search engine. Simultaneously, Google is actively expanding AI applications in other fields, such as cloud computing, advertising, and autonomous driving, striving to maintain a leading position in the AI era.

3.3. Competitor Pursuit: Bing’s Challenge and Google’s Advantages

Microsoft’s Bing, as Google’s main competitor in the search engine domain, has also been continuously exerting efforts in recent years, attempting to take a share of the market. Microsoft is not only increasing technological investment but also actively collaborating with OpenAI, integrating AI technologies like ChatGPT into the Bing search engine, aiming to seize market share through differentiated competition.

However, despite Bing’s progress in the AI ​​search field, currently, it still struggles to shake Google’s core advantages in the search engine market. Data shows that Bing’s global market share remains in the single digits, a stark contrast to Google’s nearly 90% market share. This again confirms Google’s powerful search engine user stickiness and network effects, as well as its long-term accumulated competitive advantages.

Summary: Google’s search engine dominance remains solid and is difficult to shake in the short term. The rise of AI search tools, while posing certain challenges to traditional search businesses, also provides new development opportunities for Google. With its strong technological strength, massive data resources, and proactive strategic response, Google is expected to maintain its leadership in the search engine field in the AI ​​era and even achieve greater breakthroughs in broader technology domains. For investors, Google’s dominance in the search engine domain remains a cornerstone of its long-term investment value.

4. Financial Performance: A Robust “Money-Making Machine”

Google (Alphabet) has long demonstrated outstanding profitability, with a business model built upon diversified revenue streams from advertising, cloud computing, AI technology, and hardware. In 2023, Google’s total revenue approached $300 billion, maintaining steady growth compared to previous years.

4.1. Key Profitability Data Highlights:

  • Operating Profit Margin as High as 32%: This metric showcases the company’s advantages in cost control and economies of scale. In comparison, Microsoft’s operating profit margin is approximately 42%, while Meta’s is around 30%. Although Google’s is lower than Microsoft’s, it still leads most technology companies.
  • Net Profit Margin Reaching 28%: This means that for every $1 of revenue, approximately $0.28 ultimately converts to net profit, indicating not only strong revenue growth but also highly efficient cost management.

Furthermore, Google’s profitability is also reflected in its Free Cash Flow (FCF). Free cash flow is a crucial indicator of a company’s actual earning power, and Google’s FCF has remained between $60 billion and $80 billion in recent years, ensuring ample flexibility for technological research and development, strategic investments, and shareholder returns.

4.2. Analysis of Key Financial Indicators:

Alphabet’s balance sheet also demonstrates robust stability, ensuring the company can maintain strong risk resistance in market changes and industry competition.

  • Low Debt Ratio, Sound Financials: Google’s total debt ratio is lower compared to other technology giants, with long-term debt accounting for less than 10% of the balance sheet, significantly lower than many competitors. This implies that the company can sustain business growth without relying on external financing during market volatility.
  • Accounts Payable Exceed Accounts Receivable, Obvious Supply Chain Advantages: Google’s total accounts payable reached $59.8 billion, while accounts receivable totaled $52.3 billion. This indicates the company possesses strong bargaining power in both upstream and downstream segments of the supply chain. Higher accounts payable implies Google can use supplier funds to operate, while lower accounts receivable represents the company’s ability to quickly recover funds, thereby optimizing cash flow management.
  • High Retained Earnings: Google has consistently maintained a high level of retained earnings over time, meaning the company can continuously reinvest through its own funds rather than relying on external capital. High retained earnings are also a key pillar for sustained and steady corporate growth.

4.3. Valuation Levels: An Undervalued Quality Asset

Despite Google’s robust profitability, its valuation level has not experienced excessive inflation over the long term, and even in some phases, it has been undervalued by the market.

  • Current Valuation Analysis: Price-to-Earnings Ratio (P/E) of Approximately 23x: Compared to its historical average (past five-year P/E typically ranging from 25-30x), the current P/E ratio is at a lower level. This suggests that while Google maintains good growth momentum, the market remains relatively cautious in its valuation. In comparison, Microsoft’s P/E is above 30x, and Meta’s is around 25x, making Google’s valuation appear more attractive.
  • Price-to-Sales Ratio (P/S) of Approximately 6-6.5x: P/S ratio measures the ratio of company revenue to market capitalization. This value indicates that the market’s pricing of Google’s revenue is still within a reasonable range, without the tech stock bubble seen during the pandemic.

4.4. Market Sentiment Impact and Investment Opportunities

Recently, due to market concerns about AI search potentially impacting Google’s advertising business, as well as an overall tech stock correction, Google’s stock price has seen some pullback. This, conversely, presents a buy-the-dip opportunity for long-term investors. Considering that the company is still actively investing in AI and cloud computing, and its core advertising business remains stable, the current valuation level may be underestimated by the market.

5. Investment Strategy: Value Investing as a Beacon, Illuminating Google’s Buying Opportunities

Having thoroughly analyzed Google’s fundamentals and market competitiveness, you should now have a clear understanding of Google’s long-term investment value. Next, we will focus on specific investment strategies and buying opportunities to provide more actionable guidance for your investment decisions.

5.1. Long-Term Value Choice: Google is the Best Destination for Patient Capital

Google (Alphabet) is not a thematic stock for short-term speculation but a high-quality core asset worthy of long-term holding and the best destination for patient capital. Therefore, for investors with a long-term investment horizon, Google is an ideal allocation target. Long-term holding allows you to fully enjoy the dividends of Google’s sustained growth and prosperity as a tech giant in the future tech wave. The value investing strategy of “buy low, hold long” is highly aligned with Google’s investment attributes and is the optimal choice for achieving long-term, steady returns.

5.2. Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA) Strategy: Mitigating Timing Risks and Accumulating Long-Term Shares

Stock market fluctuations are unpredictable, and accurately pinpointing the bottom is nearly impossible. To mitigate timing risks and accumulate long-term shares, we recommend adopting a “Dollar-Cost Averaging” strategy. By purchasing Google stock in batches at different times and price points, you can effectively average out the purchase cost, reduce the timing risk of a single buy, and avoid the awkward scenario of going “all-in” at a high point. Simultaneously, DCA helps investors better manage their buying pace, gradually accumulating shares during price declines, laying the foundation for future long-term gains.

Combining technical analysis and the theory of distribution of chips, we provide the following price range references for Dollar-Cost Averaging:

  • Short-Term Buying Range: Around $175 and $165 – Recent Stock Price Support Levels
    • Around $175: This is one of the more apparent support levels in Google’s recent stock price trend. If the stock price retreats to this level, consider initiating a small-position tentative buy to establish an initial position.
    • Around $165: This is a stronger support level and a more attractive entry point in the short term. If the stock price further declines to around $165, consider increasing your buying volume to further add to your position.
  • Long-Term Fair Value Range: $135-$136 – Market-Recognized Long-Term Fair Value Lowland
    • $135-$136 Range: Based on the analysis of the distribution of chips, the $135-$136 range is the fair value range for Google commonly recognized by the market over the past three years, and also the area with the densest distribution of chips, implying that the market generally believes this price range to be the long-term value bottom area for Google. Should the stock price experience an extreme situation and deeply retrace to the $135-$136 range, this would be a once-in-a-lifetime long-term buying opportunity. Consider heavy buying, or even going All-in (of course, All-in should be done cautiously, and must be based on your personal risk tolerance).

It is essential to emphasize that the above price ranges are for reference only and are not absolute buy signals. Investors need to flexibly adjust their buying strategies and position control based on their own financial situation, risk preferences, and value judgments on Google. The core of Dollar-Cost Averaging lies in “batching” and “long-term,” not in precisely predicting the bottom. As long as you adhere to Dollar-Cost Averaging and long-term holding, you can effectively reduce investment risk and share in Google’s long-term growth dividends.

5.3. Options-Assisted Strategies: Icing on the Cake, Advanced Tools to Enhance Returns (Suitable for Experienced Investors)

For investors with some options knowledge and risk tolerance, consider using options strategies to supplement stock investments, enhance investment returns while controlling risk, and build a more flexible investment portfolio. However, it is imperative to remember that options strategies are “auxiliary” means, while holding stocks is the “core” strategy. Never put the cart before the horse by pursuing options income and neglecting the essence of long-term investment.

Here are two option-assisted strategies suitable for Google stock:

  • Selling Put Options – Enhance Returns, Reduce Costs, Awaiting Optimal Entry Points

    • Strategy Principle: Sell put options at a price (strike price) you believe Google stock is unlikely to fall below, and collect the premium paid by the buyer. If, at expiration, the stock price is above the strike price, the option expires worthless, and the premium is kept as income, effectively enhancing the return on holding cash. If, at expiration, the stock price is below the strike price, the option buyer has the right to sell you the stock at the strike price, and you are obligated to purchase the stock at the strike price, effectively buying Google stock at a lower cost.

    • Suitable Scenarios: When you are optimistic about Google’s long-term investment value but believe the short-term stock price may fluctuate or slightly decline, you can adopt a strategy of selling put options. Especially when the stock price is near key support levels (e.g., $175, $165), selling put options can both earn premium income and prepare for potentially buying stocks at a lower cost in the future.

    • Risk Warning: The greatest risk of selling put options is that if the stock price drops sharply, you will be forced to buy stocks at the strike price, potentially facing book losses. Therefore, the choice of strike price is crucial. Be sure to select a buying price you are willing to accept and control your position size to avoid over-exposure to risk. This strategy is suitable for investors who have an in-depth understanding of Google’s fundamentals and possess a certain risk tolerance.

  • Buying Call Options – Small Capital for High Returns, Amplifying Upside Potential

    • Strategy Principle: When you believe Google stock has significant upside potential in the near future, you can buy call options. Buying call options only requires paying a small premium. If the stock price rises as expected, the value of the call option will also increase rapidly. You can choose to sell the option for profit or exercise it to buy stocks at a lower price.

    • Suitable Scenarios: When you judge that Google’s stock price has fallen to an undervalued region (e.g., around $135-$136) and believe the probability of future price increase is high, consider buying long-term call options. Use a smaller capital cost to aim for high returns from stock price increases, amplifying investment returns.

    • Risk Warning: The greatest risk of buying call options is that if the stock price does not rise as expected or does not reach your profit target before option expiration, the option value will continuously decay, and you may ultimately lose the entire premium. Therefore, buying call options is a high-risk, high-return speculative strategy, only suitable for small capital attempts and should not be used for heavily leveraged bets. This strategy is suitable for investors with a higher risk appetite and a certain ability to judge Google’s future stock price trends.

Again, options strategies are a “double-edged sword.” Used properly, they can add icing to the cake and enhance returns; used improperly, they may magnify risks and cause losses. For investors unfamiliar with options trading, exercise caution and avoid blindly following trends. The most prudent investment strategy remains long-term holding of Google stock, with options serving only as auxiliary tools to be flexibly utilized in specific situations.

Summary

Overall, Google exhibits strong profitability, stable cash flow, reasonable or even undervalued valuation levels, and a healthy balance sheet, making it a high-quality technology stock suitable for long-term investment. The current pullback in its stock price is more influenced by short-term factors rather than a deterioration in the company’s fundamentals. Therefore, for long-term investors, Google’s financial stability provides a strong margin of safety, and buying on dips when valuations are relatively low may be a highly attractive investment strategy.
